Analytical Context

Rate-Lock-In Effect expand_more

The spread between the avg outstanding mortgage rate (~3.8%) and the prevailing market rate suppresses existing-home turnover regardless of price trends — critical context for interpreting low inventory in 2024–2026.

Median Price Caveat expand_more

Median sale price movement can reflect either appreciation OR compositional shift (more high-end homes selling). Always pair median price with price-per-sq-ft and a repeat-sales index to disentangle the two effects.

Inventory Seasonality expand_more

Active inventory is highly seasonal — always compare YoY, not month-over-month. Rising inventory + flat/rising sales = healthy expansion. Rising inventory + falling sales = classic early signal of a market turn.

Mortgage Rate Sensitivity expand_more

Each 1% increase in mortgage rates reduces purchasing power by approximately 10–11%. Track the 30-year fixed conforming rate alongside the 10-year Treasury — the spread between them widens in periods of credit stress.
