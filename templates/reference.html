1. Supply Indicators

Months of Supply (MOS) / Months of Inventory

4. Affordability & Financial Stress

{% for title, body in [ ("NAR Housing Affordability Index", "Whether a median-income family can qualify for a median-priced home. Values above 100 = family qualifies; below 100 = family falls short."), ("Price-to-Income Ratio", "Median home price ÷ median household income. U.S. historical norm: 3–4×. Sustained readings above 5× signal affordability stress and elevated correction risk."), ("Price-to-Rent Ratio", "Median home price ÷ median annual rent. < 15× = buying is cheaper; 15–20× = borderline; > 20× = renting may be more economical. One of the most useful cross-market metrics. San Francisco and San Jose: ~45× and 40× (early 2026). Detroit: ~8×."), ("Mortgage Rate Sensitivity", "Each 1% increase in mortgage rates reduces purchasing power by ~10–11%. Track the 30-year fixed conforming rate alongside the 10-year Treasury — the spread between them widens in periods of credit stress."), ("Rate-Lock-In Effect", "Tracks the spread between the avg outstanding mortgage rate (~3.8%) and the prevailing market rate. A wide spread suppresses existing-home turnover regardless of price trends — critical context for interpreting low inventory in 2024–2026."), ("Mortgage Delinquency & Foreclosure Rates", "Lagging stress indicators — foreclosures spike after economic distress, not during it. Useful for confirming a downturn, not predicting one. Standard sources: MBA National Delinquency Survey and ATTOM foreclosure data."), ] %}
